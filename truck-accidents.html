Commercial Truck Accident Claims

Collisions involving commercial trucks, tractor-trailers, box trucks, and delivery vehicles are categorically different from standard car accidents. The injuries are often catastrophic, the liable parties are multiple, and the defense resources are substantial. Trucking companies carry significant insurance and deploy legal teams immediately after serious accidents. You need representation that matches that level of preparation.

Federal Regulations and Carrier Liability

Commercial trucking is governed by the Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration (FMCSA), which sets strict standards for driver hours, vehicle maintenance, cargo loading, and licensing. Violations of these regulations — hours-of-service logs, mandatory rest breaks, equipment inspections — can establish negligence per se. We immediately subpoena electronic logging device (ELD) data, maintenance records, dispatch communications, and driver qualification files before they are altered or destroyed.

Multiple Parties May Be Liable

Unlike car accidents, truck crashes often involve several potentially liable parties: the truck driver, the carrier or trucking company, the cargo loader or shipper, the vehicle or parts manufacturer, and in some cases, a leasing company. Identifying all responsible parties early is critical to maximizing recovery and ensuring that judgments can actually be collected. Arden Law conducts a thorough investigation to trace every chain of liability.

The Scale of Damages

Truck accident victims frequently sustain catastrophic injuries — spinal cord damage, traumatic brain injuries, multiple fractures, amputations, and injuries requiring years of rehabilitation. The damages in these cases are correspondingly large, covering not just current medical bills but lifetime care costs, lost earning capacity, and the profound impact on quality of life. We work with medical experts, economists, and vocational rehabilitation specialists to document the full scope of what you have lost.

Why Timing Matters

Evidence in truck accident cases disappears quickly. Trucking companies may repair vehicles, lose dash cam footage, and overwrite ELD data within days. Arden Law moves immediately to preserve evidence and, where necessary, obtains court orders to prevent spoliation. Acting fast is not just strategic — it is essential.
